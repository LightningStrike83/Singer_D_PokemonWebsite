Tips?

Usage Tips:

  • Hold the image near the top or bottom of the webpage to scroll up/down.
  • Regional Pokemon can be found underneath the standard Pokedex Pokemon.
  • Classic Pokedex has 151 slots.
  • Legends Pokedex has 242 slots.
  • Modern Pokedex has 400 slots.
  • You can return the Pokemon to their original positions on the left side!
  • Press F5/refresh the page to reset the Pokedexes~

Pokedex Trends and Tips:

  • Every Pokedex has at least 3 starter Pokemon: Grass, Fire, and Water.
  • Every Pokedex has at least 3 Legendary Pokemon.
  • Every Pokedex has at least 1 regional bug. Eg. Caterpie, Sewaddle, Blipbug.
  • Every Pokedex has at least 1 regional bird. Eg. Pidgey, Starly, Fletchling.
  • Every Pokedex has at least 1 early route normal type. Eg. Wooloo, Sentret, Bidoof.
  • Every Pokedex has at least 1 pseudo-legendary. Eg. Dragonite, Metagross, Garchomp.
  • Every Pokedex has at least 1 Mythical Pokemon. Eg. Mew, Jirachi, Marshadow.
  • Every Pokedex has at least 1 "Pikachu clone". Eg. Pachirisu, Morpeko, Pawmot.
  • Since Generation 2, Legendaries have usually come in pairs. Eg. Groudon/Kyogre, Zacian/Zamazenta, Reshiram/Zekrom.
  • Since Generation 3, there is usually a third legend that connects to the paired legends. Eg. Giratina, Necrozma, Zygarde.
  • Most Pokedexes have separate legendary trios/quads. Eg. The Regis, The Tapus, The Forces Of Nature.
  • Psyduck, Golduck, Magnemite, Magneton, Heracross, Drifloon, Drifblim, Riolu, Lucario, Weavile, Magnezone, Leafeon, Glaceon, Basculin, Zorua, Zoroark, Sylveon, Goomy, Sliggoo, and Goodra have appeared in every regional Pokedex (counting DLC dexes and using New Unova's Pokedex) since their debut.
